Here's the grand finale — ATP Synthase! All those protons that got pumped into the intermembrane space have built up massive pressure, and now they want out.
The only way through?
ATP Synthase.

As protons flood back through it, they physically spin the protein like a turbine. That spinning motion powers the attachment of a phosphate group onto ADP — and just like that, you've got ATP!
All that electron shuffling and proton pumping was building toward this one moment.
